# Analysis Summary

## Overview
This analysis reviews prenatal care utilization using patient demographics, income level, language barriers, and birth outcome data. The project demonstrates how SQL can be used to identify patterns that may reflect healthcare access issues or disparities.

## Key Findings

### 1. Prenatal Visits by Race/Ethnicity
The analysis showed differences in average prenatal visits across racial and ethnic groups. Hispanic patients had the highest average number of prenatal visits, while Black and Asian patients had lower averages in the sample dataset.

This may suggest potential differences in access, care utilization, insurance coverage, transportation, or other social factors.

### 2. Prenatal Visits by Income Level
Middle-income patients had the highest average number of prenatal visits, while low-income patients had fewer visits on average. This supports the idea that socioeconomic factors can influence access to prenatal care.

### 3. Language Barriers
Patients with language barriers were identified as an important group to monitor. Language differences may affect communication, understanding of care instructions, appointment adherence, and navigation of the healthcare system.

## Recommendations
- Improve outreach for patients with lower prenatal visit frequency
- Add translation and multilingual support services
- Monitor patients with socioeconomic barriers such as transportation, childcare, or work leave limitations
- Use dashboards to track prenatal care utilization trends over time
- Expand the dataset to support stronger statistical analysis

## Conclusion
This project shows how relational databases and SQL analysis can support healthcare decision-making. By organizing prenatal care data and analyzing utilization trends, care teams can better identify gaps, improve outreach, and support maternal and infant health outcomes.
